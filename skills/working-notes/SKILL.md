## The Aim

Usually, agents can handle simple tasks they are trained on and familiar with. But for many tasks — even general ones — they tend to be lazy and fail to reach a decent level of thoroughness, especially when there is a lot of confusion clouding the task. This skill introduces working notes: an approach for completing any task decently, at a human level.

## The Working-Notes Method

Maintain an editable working-notes file (`.md`, `.txt`, etc.).

The notes can include the following:
- The aim or underlying meaning of the task or request
- Key factors for a thorough completion (checkpoints, concerns spotted)
- Confusions (from anything: the user, the task content, the situation)
- ... (define other parts based on the situation)

The notes should contain key points that are critical but easy to miss. You do not need to write down anything you would never get wrong. Leaving the working notes empty — or not using them at all — is better when there is nothing worth noting. Minimal but information-rich is the golden standard.

While exploring or doing the task, you can edit the notes: cross out resolved confusions, update reminders, etc. The notes act as a "mental stabilizer" — helping you store critical points and free up your attention.
